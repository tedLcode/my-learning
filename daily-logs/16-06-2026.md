## 📚 What I Studied Today
- Topic: Customer Journey Modeling
  - What I understood: Customer evolution can be represented as timeline-based streams that combine financial activities (portfolio changes, product adoption) with interaction data (clicks, calls, app usage, branch visits)
  - What I understood: Interaction patterns are not just metadata — they actively influence product adoption and retention behavior, so the two streams need to be modeled together, not separately
  - What I understood: Segmentation (by generation, asset bracket, line of business) is not just a reporting filter — it's the lens through which you generate personalized, actionable insights

- Topic: Process Mining
  - What I understood: Process mining differs from traditional manual analysis by ingesting raw event data and using graph-based algorithms to automatically surface hidden patterns and possible customer journeys — you don't define the paths upfront, the data reveals them
  - What I understood: The output is typically an interactive visualization — business users can explore different journey paths, filter by customer segment, and derive insights without needing to write queries
  - What I understood: This approach is powerful because it captures the actual paths customers take, including unexpected or uncommon ones, rather than the idealized paths a business assumes

- Topic: Business Problem Framing for Analytics
  - What I understood: Framing a business question correctly (e.g., "which customers are likely to churn in the next 90 days" vs. "show me customer activity") is what determines whether an analytics solution is actionable or just informational
  - What I understood: The gap between a business question and a technical implementation is often larger than it looks — understanding the business scenario deeply is a prerequisite to designing the right data model
